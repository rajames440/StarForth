# Inspirations & Acknowledgements
“This project assumes physical invariants exist, even when observations differ.”

This project is part of a long lineage of thinkers, builders, engineers,
mathematicians, scientists, and system designers who shared a refusal
to lie about reality, measurement, time, or systems.

What follows is not hagiography.
It is lineage.

---

## Ancient & Classical Foundations

- **Democritus** — Material reality exists independent of belief; atomism without instruments.
- **Epicurus** — Physics as a cure for superstition and fear.
- **Lucretius** — Clear documentation of a material universe; no divine runtime.
- **Aristotle** — Systematic taxonomy and causality, even when conclusions were wrong.
- **Euclid** — Axioms first; everything else must follow or be rejected.
- **Archimedes** — Mathematics applied directly to physical mechanisms.
- **Heron of Alexandria** — Early automation, feedback, and control systems.
- **Hippocrates** — Removed gods from medicine; observation over invocation.
- **Thucydides** — History without divine excuses; incentives and consequences.
- **Marcus Aurelius** — Acceptance of entropy and duty without illusion.
- **Claudius (Roman Emperor)** — Administrative competence over performance; strategic invisibility.

## Major Footnote — Frequently Misinterpreted Contributors

The individuals listed below are acknowledged strictly for their
first-principles reasoning, mathematical formalism, and measurement
constraints — not for popular, metaphysical, or narrative interpretations.

- **Erwin Schrödinger** — Formalism and a warning about metaphor leakage.
- **Werner Heisenberg** — Measurement bounds, not ontological fog.
- **Paul Dirac** — Well, G − m Ψ =0. What else is there to say?

Popular explanations, mystical interpretations, and metaphor-driven
reframings of their work are explicitly rejected by this project.

If you recognize these figures only through pop science,
you do not yet understand why they are listed here.
