\section{L8 Attractor Heartbeat Analysis}

\textbf{Date:} 9 December 2025. \textbf{Experiment:} StarForth L8 Attractor
Map. \textbf{Runs:} 180 (6 workloads $\times$ 30 replicates).
\textbf{Total heartbeat ticks:} 4{,}351.

\subsection{Key Findings}

\begin{description}
  \item[Workload-Independent Convergence.]
        ANOVA on tick count versus workload: $F(5,\,174) = 0.983$,
        $p = 0.43$. Convergence time is not significantly affected by
        workload type, confirming deterministic attractor behavior.

  \item[Timing Consistency.]
        Mean tick interval: 70--75\,$\mu$s across all workloads.
        Coefficient of variation below 5\% at steady state.

  \item[Workload-Specific Heat Signatures.]
        Each workload exhibits a distinct steady-state word heat signature.
        VOLATILE showed the highest intra-workload variance
        ($\sigma = 8.16 \times 10^{-5}$); TEMPORAL, the lowest
        ($\sigma = 1.76 \times 10^{-5}$).
\end{description}

\subsection{Conclusions}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The L8 attractor exhibits deterministic convergence across all six
        workload types.
  \item Heartbeat timing is highly stable (CV $< 5\%$ at steady state).
  \item Zero crashes in 180 runs confirm the memory-safety fixes applied
        prior to this run.
  \item The OMNI workload (7$\times$ standard computational load) served as
        a successful stress test, confirming that timing stability is
        independent of raw computational intensity.
\end{enumerate}
