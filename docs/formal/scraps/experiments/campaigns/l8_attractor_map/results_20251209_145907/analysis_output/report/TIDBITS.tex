\section{L8 Attractor Map: Notable Observations from the 9 December 2025 Run}

The following observations were recorded during analysis of the 180-run
L8 attractor dataset (results\_20251209\_145907). They represent
qualitative findings that supplement the formal statistical report.
Several have since been partially explained; others remain open.

\begin{description}
  \item[The Jitter Mystery.]
        Estimated jitter constituted 86\% of the tick interval. This is not
        measurement noise. The heartbeat operates near a fundamental
        uncertainty limit: the system cannot be more timing-precise without
        violating something inherent in its adaptive dynamics.

  \item[TRANSITION as a Phase-Change Region.]
        TRANSITION variability (CV $\approx 22.5\%$) was four times higher
        than STABLE (CV $\approx 5\%$) and produced the most anomalies
        (10 of 28 total). TRANSITION is not a workload in the ordinary
        sense; it is a critical point where the system is genuinely
        unstable, analogous to a melting boundary.

  \item[VOLATILE Warms Over Time.]
        Mean tick interval for VOLATILE increased from 74.6\,$\mu$s (first
        tick) to 79.9\,$\mu$s (last tick), a $+7.1\%$ rise. This
        thermodynamic-style accumulation was not expected and gives the name
        ``VOLATILE'' an unintended prophetic quality.

  \item[Bimodal Hot-Word Distribution.]
        The hot-word count distribution was bimodal: mode at 0 (most
        ticks have zero hot words) with a secondary cluster at 6--7.
        The behavior resembles quantum ground-state versus excited-state
        switching rather than a gradual heating curve.

  \item[Golden Ratio Appearance.]
        A tick-interval ratio of approximately 1.583 (close to
        $\varphi \approx 1.618$) appeared in DIVERSE at tick 13.
        In chaotic systems the golden ratio appears in bifurcation
        cascades and resonant frequencies; this single observation
        is suggestive of self-organized criticality but requires
        replication before any claim can be made.

  \item[Orbiting, Not Settling.]
        Convergence to a stable value took 4--5 ticks, but the system
        continued oscillating afterward. The L8 attractor finds an orbit
        around the attractor rather than converging to a fixed point;
        this is genuine strange-attractor behavior.

  \item[Instrumentation Gap.]
        All delta metrics (cache hits, bucket hits, word executions,
        predictions) were zero throughout the run. The heartbeat was
        measuring temporal rhythm only, not computational events.
        This gap was subsequently identified as Priority 1 for the
        next instrumentation pass.

  \item[Fixed Window Width.]
        Window width registered as a constant 4096 throughout the run.
        This was later understood to be an instrumentation artifact:
        the adaptive window was changing, but \texttt{effective\_window\_size}
        was not exported to the heartbeat CSV. Adding this metric was
        flagged as critical follow-up work.
\end{description}
